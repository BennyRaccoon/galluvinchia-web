## Introduction
I have been the Dungeon Master of several groups for the last 6 years, and it is something that has always called to me. Hearing the call sometimes is difficult, as the Dungeon Master is the master puppeteer behind the world, often the creator of it, and the one moving the gears in the story. 

It might involve a lot of work and, more importantly, responsibilities.

But fear not! I hope this small guide helps you push yourself into a proficient Dungeon Master, or at least, a fun and fearless one!


\column


## The basics
:
### The Role of <br>the Dungeon Master
You are the narrator, the referee, the creative spark that guides the world of any tabletop RPG experience. The Dungeon Master (or Game Master) as the master of the rules, has to be confident and capable to keep the game stable and give an aura of reliability. 

This way, the game can always keep going and growing within its frame. Remember you are the law, but also your objective is to have a fun game with epic fights and awesome stories.

### How to play
:
#### The flow
1. The Dungeon Master describes a scene
2. Players interact with the scene
3. The Dungeon Master describes the outcome guided by the use of dice rolls if they are needed.

#### The goal
It is important that you are aware of the goal of any campaign you begin, and what the final scenario that you are seeking. In the case of the adventure included at the end of this book, the final goal is to save the island from an impending doom that might affect all Galluvinchia. 

This goal doesn't necessarily have to be known by the characters, which in this case is hidden almost to the very end. And they are enticed to first search for where the storms are originating. 

Then, they investigate the mines and the ruins within. 

And, finally, chase the cultists into their hideout and stop the summoning circle, if they are able to, to realize that there is a bigger danger lurking from another world.


##### Types of DMs
There are mainly two different types of Dungeons Masters. This matter can go deep into the history of Dungeons & Dragons and other tabletop RPGs, but this is a bit of a summary:

###### OSR: Classic Dungeon Mastering
This type of Dungeon Mastering is focused in a more simulationist, world reacting storytelling. The DM focuses in thinking of how the world would react, and describe so accordingly. **Adventurers can become heroes, but they will have to earn it**. The world can be lethal and very challenging.

###### Neo-trad: Traditional Dungeon Mastering
On this occasion, the fate of the players is set to follow a destiny, in many cases, become heroes. 

It is not about how the world would react, but on how the DM, with the players, can make the best story together. It is focused in narrative more than adventuring. This is the style of popular shows like Critical Role.
\column
##### My type of DMing
My type of DMing, and the one that I recommend as it is the one I consider more fun to master and also to play, is based in OSR, but with some sprinkles of Neo-trad.

**I blend the challenge and realism of OSR with the narrative power of Neo-trad.**

I will run the story as a simulation, the world reacts not only to the players but also the NPCs have their own agendas and plans, and they will try to chase them as much as possible, as people, in real life, would do.

At the same time, I expect players to become heroes. I want them to succeed and, although I don't twist the world to fit their characters' decisions, I heavily work in their background, incorporating elements in the future that will lead to an emotional resolution of their characters.

Even if something is outside the rules, I would allow it as long as it is very sporadically and does not create imbalance between the different characters and the world.

##### My Dungeon Master commandments
**1 The adventure** :: Prepare a living world that works by itself but is welcoming of your adventurers.
**2 The adventurers**:: Get everyone involved and allow them to grow in your adventure; feed their creativity with the options from your world!
**3 The goal**:: Overcome challenges and allow the players to fail. Not all stories are built by becoming heroes, but also by overcoming our failures.
**4 Be fair**:: Keep the world consistent and your rulings clear; fairness builds trust in the game’s reality.
**5 Respect each other's time**:: Something very important to me is to have a strong commitment to the group and from the group to you. You spend a lot of time preparing, they should respect it, be punctual and be clear on their expectations and planning.

#### The rules of the game

##### The attributes
The world of Dungeons and Dragons is governed by attributes that translate both challenges and abilities from the real world into the table. These attributes are:

| Attribute | Explanation |
|:------------------|:-----|
| Strength          | How strong is a character,  how much can they carry, or how hard can they hit.     |
| Dexterity         | How agile, from avoiding dangers to throwing something at a distance.     |
| Constitution      | The health or how the character can deal with poisons or other effects on the body. |
| Intelligence      | How smart you are to solve puzzles, to remember riddles, to cast magic, or to investigate a situation or room.     |
| Wisdom            | This represents subconscious knowledge and awareness; this is how smartly you interact with the world around you.     |
| Charisma          | How easily you can talk and convince other people, the power of the will.     |

##### Dice Check
To resolve the events happening in the world, the players must roll a dice check by rolling a 20-sided die (d20 from now on) and add the proper modifiers. 

**For example**:: there is a rune warding a lever, and your players want to investigate it. <br>They could try to roll an Arcana Check to try to decipher the meaning of such a magic ward.  <br>You should allow for them to roll an Ability Check. <br>  <br>They will roll a d20, check their score in Arcana, and add proficiency if they are proficient.<br> <br>You, as a Dungeon Master, will have to assess if the roll is enough to achieve their task. The higher the number they have to match, the more difficult the task is.
\column


###### Ability Check
When your players want to achieve something that requires focusing on that ability, they might need to roll for an Ability Check. 

Do they want to investigate a room, persuade the guard to let them go, or read some runes? Sliding down a slippery slope? Make them cast the dice for it! **Initiative rolls** are also Ability Checks.

###### Saving Throw
From spells, traps, or any other inconvenience, players and creatures have to try to save themselves from the unexpected. **Death Saving Throws** are also here.

###### Attack Check
When you attack, you will have to roll and add the proper ability modifiers to the Attack Roll that you are doing, as well as proficiency if you are proficient in the spell or weapon you are using. 

This roll has to match the opponent's Armor Class (AC); if they do, they can roll for damage! 

#### The adventure
Find a balance between preparing for the session and being able to improvise on the spot. At the beginning it might be difficult, but I believe that creativity is a muscle, and the more we practice it, the better we will be at coming up with creative solutions. 

Remember, not only this book, but any tabletop RPG guide, they are just a crane to help you stand up, but you still have to use your legs to walk.

##### Creating Memorable Characters
Non-player characters (NPCs) are the blood of any campaign. 

Creating interesting ones can be difficult at first, and then it can become difficult to create big differences between them.
:
In my experience, it is way more important to create unique characters and try to remember their basics so the responsibility to the amount of interaction falls in their players' hands.

Not all characters need to be special, but there are some that have a bigger weight in the lore or hide stories behind them.

For the rest and unusual random npcs, keep at hand a bunch of names for characters that you haven't thought of, and if they are not essential, don't give a huge description.

Remember you have to guide your players, at least a bit, into your prepared content.

#### The Action

##### One round: the 6 seconds
Every turn is supposed to reflect 6 seconds of interaction.
The resources of the creatures are called actions, and in general, everyone has one action, one bonus action, one free action, one reaction and one movement per turn.

Besides these, you can always improvise, and this is just the basics of what all characters might be able to do.
For spell casters, each spell occupies one of these resources, so they will not be specifically disclosed here.
##### Light

**Bright**:: everyone sees normally.

**Dim light**:: targets are lightly obscured: Disadvantage on sight-based Perception.

**Darkness**:: you are Blinded.

Characters that have Darkvision, can see dim light as bright and darkness as dim light.

##### Terrain
**Normal terrain**:: 1 ft is 1 ft travelled
**Difficult terrain**:: 2 ft is 1 ft travelled, like walking in a dense jungle or in ice.

##### Combat
The combat starts when both, friends and foes have noticed each other, or when the first action on each other is going to happen.

Sometimes they are sneaky, or they have a plan that they want to make in order and it is better to make a sequence of actions before the combat starts, or instead of!

Otherwise...
###### Roll for Initiative!

Everyone, including your NPCs, need to roll for Initiative. 
If you want, you can get help from the players to keep the tracking in order, as you have to also track everything else.

Tracking the turns is also important, so you can keep bringing events every turn, as long as they are relevant.

If **there are many actors in place**, sometimes it can become relevant to take turns between players and enemies, so they can do all at the same time and not entangle a lot the Initiative order.

\column
###### Movement
**★ Move**:: Just moving, it doesn't need to be all at once
**Drop prone**:: You will have to crawl and apply the Prone condition to you, but you will be more difficult to hit with ranged attacks.
**Stand up**:: Spend half of your movement by standing up and removing the Prone condition.

###### Actions
**★ Attack**:: Make a melee or ranged attack
**Dash**:: Move again
**Disengage**:: Avoid opportunity attacks
**Grapple**:: Try to apply Grapple to a creature
**Dodge**:: Gives Disadvantage on Attack Rolls against you (if you can see the attacker) and grants you Advantage on Dexterity Saving Throws
**Shove**:: Try to push an enemy
**Hide**:: Try to hide and get Advantage if successful
**Help**:: Help with an Ability Check or Attack Roll (gives Advantage)
**Ready an action**:: Set a trigger for an action, which will use your reaction. Example: "If an enemy gets into reach, I will attack him".

###### Bonus actions
**Off hand attack**:: If you attacked with a light weapon in your main hand, you can use your off hand to attack without adding your ability modifier to the damage roll.
**Other**:: There are no other standard bonus actions, but many classes have their own special ones.

###### Free actions
**Quick interaction**:: Interactions with objects that don't take forever or require a roll. Dropping a weapon and drawing another is a free action, but not sheathing the weapon to store it.  
**Drop concentration**:: To stop a spell that you are casting, you can do it any time.
**Communication**:: Talk is okay as long as it is not a 300-word monologue!

###### Reactions
**Opportunity attacks**:: When an enemy leaves your melee reach, you can make an opportunity attack.
**Readied action**:: Uses your readied action if you used your action on it.

###### Legendary Actions
Bosses and powerful creatures might have legendary actions, which you can use 3 for each round! Don't forget to use them, they are there to be used and balanced!

## Adventure Planning
Explore how to create an immersive setting and weave it into story arcs.

From brainstorming and map-making to developing intriguing plot points, this section guides you in building memorable worlds.


### Storytelling Techniques
As you get better as a Dungeon Master, you will probably want to improve your storytelling. In some cases, describing places or people are a bit difficult because we focus too much on the details. 

Instead, try to focus more on the feelings, *"the vibe"* you get from the place or from the NPC you are describing. What's the aura the seller gives? Does he look happy, trustworthy? Is the place dark and grim? Or is it so crowded that it is difficult to walk through?


Also, don't hesitate to use images to help you describe places or people; that's the reason all important NPCs have a portrait. I want to be able to tell my players what they look like without ambiguity.

### Session Planning
Effective organization makes running a game smoother. This section covers various approaches to session prep, from loose outlines to more structured plans.

**If there is a place to be strict, it is in session planning;** after all, time is the only thing we only spend in our lifetime…

#### Session 0
Session zero is where you pull together the goal and the setting of the campaign. It is a great place to meet each other for the first time. Also, it is the best moment to know about the players' personal goals and what both you as a DM and the players have in common, and what are the different boundaries that none want to reach. 

For example, is player versus player possible? Is it expected to be a heroic adventure, or is an evil route possible as well? What is the technology, and how are the cities in the setting? Is magic common?
:
For this, I have created a checklist at the end of this chapter, as well as a printable version in the Vault at thebuncult.com/vault, so you can go through it with your players and you can find common ground together.

#### Beginning of a session
There is always some chit-chat before the session, but be clear and loud, and say… **What happened last time?** And this will get the team into the game already.

#### End of a session
It is better to close a session when there is a finished task than in the middle of a fight. Avoid at all costs finishing in the middle of a fight, or it will be difficult to find the right vibe next time.


#### End of a season/adventure
If you divide your campaign into seasons, like this one is divided, 
and, if an adventure closes successfully, make it possible for characters to find rest or to even leave the adventure and find peace. 

Sometimes players will get tired of characters, characters will get tired of adventuring, and there needs to be a space where they find themselves as achieved, even if there is some kind of continuation.

For example, at the end of the adventure in this book, people can decide to continue the quest that the goddess gave them or just consider their adventure finished.

#### Conflict Resolution
Human nature can bring some issues sometimes, and even more if you are starting to play with people that don't know each other, as it is sometimes difficult to match people in the game. Remember, it is a game, but it is a game that you have decided to invest a lot of time in.


##### Problematic players
There is a distinction to be made: players' problems and problematic players. 

Is there someone that keeps interrupting the rest? Not allowing the spotlight to reach everyone? Decides to sabotage their party or makes the game about themselves?

Be able to draw a line. One pirate doesn't transform the boat into a pirate ship, but it has to be detained before the ship sinks.

##### Rule disputes
Rules in Dungeons and Dragons aren't always as clear as they seem to be, and often they will not fit all the situations.

Remember that, although you have the last word on how things work, people might have invested too much to get the outcome they have prepared for.

There are two sentences that can make things easier for you and the players:
**What's your plan?**:: This sentence will help the team to reveal the full scope of the plan, and you can guide them on what can be possible or not and the roll checks they need to achieve it.
**Rule of cool.**:: You will allow things to happen this way, but from now on, the effect of that rule or spell is going to be in another way. You can even think about it before the next session and establish the ruling for the future.


##### Scheduling
It might be difficult to find the perfect time for everyone to play; that's why it is important to take this part seriously, as it involves a lot of compromise and work to keep a group rolling, especially on your part, as the DM. 

My suggestion is to keep the dates static unless at least half of the team is missing. If half of the team is missing, it can be difficult to keep the story consistent and for everyone to feel part of the adventure.
:
Always try to keep the schedule without changes as much as possible, as well as being clear that the sessions can end half an hour earlier or later.

### Suggested additional rules
These are some of the additional rules I came up for my games with the intention of making more epic encounters and swift interactions. In many cases they also make better sense of the game or facilitate the daily work of the Dungeon Master and the sessions of the players. Use them at your own discretion.
#### Improved critical attacks 
Natural 20 means murder time! The damage is calculated by adding the maximum value of the extra dice to the normal damage roll.

###### Example with cantrip firebolt: 

| Event | Math description | Damage range
|:--|:-----------------|-------:
| Normal hit| 1d10 | 1 to 10
| Standard critical| 1d10 + 1d10| 2 to 20
| **Improved critical**| 1d10 + Max(1d10) | 11 to 20

###### Example with +3 Strength longsword attack:

| Event | Math description | Damage range
|:--|:-----------------|-------:
| Normal hit| 1d8 + Strength | 4 to 11
| Standard critical| 1d8 + 1d8 + Strength | 5 to 19 
| **Improved critical**| 1d8 + Max(1d8) + Strength | 12 to 19

:
**Reasoning:** Sometimes, when rolling a Critical Hit then the damage doesn't live up to the fun of rolling a 20 and makes that feeling to fade quickly when 3 damage is rolled. 
This way, the Critical Hit, feels critical. Obviously, this also applies to enemy creatures.
:
 I think this is a popular rule in many tables under other different names.
#### Flanking attacks 
If an enemy is already engaged in a melee fight, your character can gain a +1 bonus to their melee Attack Roll by attacking from the opposite side of the enemy. 
:
**Reasoning:** this allows to gain Advantage from other sources (like the enemy being Prone) while providing some position bonus. Enemies can flank players.

#### Dual-wield attacks 
If you are making a melee attack and you have a light melee weapon in your offhand, you can attack with both instead of using your bonus action.You don't add your ability modifier to the damage roll of the offhand.
:
**Reasoning:** simplifying dual-wield attacks, also applies for Extra Attacks.

#### Potions
You can use your potions as a bonus action.
:
**Reasoning:** potions are expensive and usually not as strong as for the cost, I think adding this quality-of-life change makes potions more accessible.

#### Resting
The party is limited to two short rests per long rest. And one long rest per day.
:
**Reasoning:** keep it simple to be able to have a better balance in session and to know what to expect of the day.

#### Leveling
Milestone leveling is superior and that's the only way it should be.
:
**Reasoning:** The stories I craft are tailored in a way that people can avoid some battles but players can also fight more enemies than expected. Trying to fix the experience points to match a path it would be impossible and it can result in turning some challenging fights to easy and bland experiences.

#### Stealth and Perception
In general, these rolls are all grouped and averaged. Critical failures or saves balance each other. If there is more fails than saves, it will literaly fail, and vice versa.

:
**Reasoning:** This works in the way that people rolling higher can help who is rolling low, teaching them or being able to notice things that would reveal the group or the group would be able to see.

#### Pushed into a wall

When a creature is pushed into the wall, it gets damage equal to 1d4 damage per each 5 feet remaining of the push.

**Example**: A creature is pushed 15 feet but after 5 feet is pushed into a wall. The creature will receive 1d4 for each remaining 5 feet, in this case, 2d4.

:
**Reasoning:** This way we get a more realistic approach to pushing damage.


#### Positioning

When a creature is blocking the path of a ranged attack, it provides half cover to the target creature. If the creature blocking the path is one size larger than the target creature, it provides Total Cover.

:
**Reasoning:** It just makes sense and makes more fun the positioning.


#### Surprise
On the first round of combat, surprising creatures go before the surprised ones. That's it.
:
**Reasoning**: In 2014, characters get a full round for free which resolves combats too quickly and it really turns the table to one side, very fast. In 2024, they tried to fix it by giving Advantage to the surprising characters, which can also be underwhelming as you might even go after the surprised ones. My way: keep it simple but still effective.

#### People thinking
People taking too long to decide what to do on their turn might get their turn skipped because their character was *“thinking“*. 

This is held to keep the fight fluid, and still offer guidance to players that have questions about how things can work.

:
**Reasoning:** Sometimes people forget they are playing a combat and if they are not focused the turns can become sluggish, which in a long combat it makes everyone's life miserable. But remember, everyone approaches the table to have fun and roleplay a character and interact with your world.

There are different types of players with different knowledge and skill levels, so this rule is not to be applied indiscriminately, but only exists to keep people focused in the combat and have some penalty if they intend to be distracted and not think in their turn before their Initiative order clocks it. Consider giving some warnings before forcing this rule.

#### Inspiration
Inspiration gives every player a random card from a standard poker deck that the player will keep facing down and without them knowing which card they got. 

When they want to use their inspiration, they can reveal their card and add it to the roll that they previously cast. Numbered cards add that number to the roll, figures count as 10. 

:
**Reasoning:** In this way, inspiration can be more exciting, without removing the part that they can still fail. Also, I would only give inspiration after milestones, in that way people can hold the card for longer and use it more tactically.

#### Standard array
To create new characters, I have decided that Standard Array is the best way to keep things consistent.

The Standard Array is assigning these values to the different attributes 15, 14, 13, 12, 10, and 8.
:
**Reasoning:** Using the Standard Array keeps thing simple and collected, reducing the friction and making everyone balanced to each other.

#### Plane Shift 
Plane Shift and other abilities to travel out of the plane have to be learned by specific means and they use a specific item or gem.

:
**Reasoning:** In this adventure there is only one realm, and there is a precise way to travel between places which is a secret for the players.

#### Lucky
Lucky feat is not available and also the lucky ability from halflings is removed.
:
**Reasoning:** To me lucky is even metagaming. Making other people re-roll is nasty and unfair.


#### Arcana
Once per long rest, you can make an Arcana Check to attempt to identify the properties of a magic item. The DC and the amount of information revealed is determined by the DM, and may vary depending on the item's rarity or obscurity.
:
**Reasoning:** Arcana is not used very often in the adventures and as the Ripple, in my setting, bathes everyone, they should be able to identify some objects.

#### History
History can be used to remember a piece of lore that they lived, witnessed, or they had read about it. As the DM, the more important you think it is for the moment you are at, the lower the difficulty. You can use this also to tell them additional lore that they don't remember or that they, as players, missed, but probably their character knows or remembers.
:
**Reasoning:** Campaigns take a lot of time and sometimes getting people into schedule can be very tricky. For this reason, it is normal that they forget stuff even if they have written it down. This can help as long as it is not abused.


#### Learning spells
Wizards learning new spells with a scroll have a discount if they have seen the spell being cast before.

**Reasoning:** This rewards exploration and also, in the way my campaign works by not using ingredients but just gold (*dinarii and aureos*) when needed, it makes more sense and ads a bit more of flavor.

#### Heroic Sacrifice 
"Heroic Sacrifice" is a new ability that can be used at any time, as long as no Death Saving Throws have been rolled for this character in the current encounter.

It is, in a way, **the players' Legendary Action**.
:
Whether you are tired of a particular character, a player can no longer participate in the game, or the player themselves wishes to create a memorable moment of heroic defeat, there is always a chance to achieve an epic moment through a heroic sacrifice!
:
Naturally, the act of heroic sacrifice should be consistent with the character's personality and contribute to the overall story.

For instance, it would be fitting for a barbarian to hold up a collapsing ceiling to protect the party or for a sorcerer to create a barrier of water to shield the party from danger before being consumed themselves. 

However, it would not make sense for these characters to be swapped to the other situation.
:
**Reasoning:** 
The purpose of this new rule is to allow for the epic exit of certain characters and reinforce the narrative of the tale. After all, we are all here to create myths and legends, aren't we?

### Heroic Ambitions
Long before the current gods, there has always been the feeling of seeking the path of glory. The champions of Galluvinchia have interwoven in their souls the beat of adventure and curiosity, and so great are their ambitions. Moved by the story of the gods, of old paladin orders, of kingdoms of giants, discovering the past is as epic as creating the future. Characters created in this setting will feel rewarded as they develop and chase greater tales than themselves.

### Flawed heroes
Like the gods of Galluvinchia, the heroes of this land are also flawed. It is important to create a character that has a flaw, and this is something that the gods themselves reveal. Many times, it is the way to relate to the god as sharing one of the flaws: Morphia can swiftly surrender to passion or openness; where virtue becomes flaw, allowing her to be seen as weak or spending time on not so critical topics. 

Aremedia's unyielding will is so strong and energetic that she might consume resources when a more tranquil pace would nurture different paths. And so, their champions might take hedonistic or exploitative flaws.
:
That being the case, all races in this book have a flaw, which is as essential as the rest of the traits for each race.

## Races
An assortment of people live in the lands of Galluvinchia. Unless they are visiting from another world, the other races are unknown to Galluvinchians, and everyone would be shocked to find them around. 

This chapter gives information about the following races existing in Galluvinchia:
- **Humans** of Galluvinchia are adaptable, ambitious, and, in the case of Galluvinchia, adept worshippers of their deities.
- **Felinfolk**, proud felines that would defy the gods if they interfere in their path to knowledge.
- **Centaurs** with their equine strength work the fields around An'Ramoda and with their big heart work in many Health Centres.
- **Minotaurs**, with the head of a glorious bull (or cow). Sometimes feared for their infamous violent stories and their strong builds, but not more than an unfounded prejudice from the past.
- **Fauns** that once emerged from the forest, they have a special connection with nature and arts.
- **Elves of the Mist** are the lost child of an old civilization. It is a rare thing to ever see one, nowadays they are growing into the realm of legend and fables.

Much like in the old Roman Empire, one can be both pragmatic and devout, recognizing that worshipping a god does not preclude practicality. 

The Romans understood that the gods could be invoked for favour in battles, prosperity in commerce, or success in personal endeavours.

Similarly, in Galluvinchia, citizens honour their deities with reverence while pursuing practical goals, leveraging divine blessings to enhance their worldly achievements. This duality reflects a sophisticated understanding that faith and pragmatism can coexist, each reinforcing the other to create a balanced and prosperous society.

And being a worshipper can be in any way; if you worship Aremedia you will value more strength and the survival of the fittest, and if you worship Morphia you will prefer a peaceful life or the pursuit of knowledge.

## The source
Most of the people in Galluvinchia have a deity they worship, and it is a very important part of everyone's life. 

A pantheon of 6 gods guides people's life, sometimes more present than not. From life, to death, from dreaming to learning, from singing to fighting, these 6 deities are formed from the actions of their believers.

## The Church of Panos
Panos, perhaps the least worshipped of the four major deities in Galluvinchia, holds the esteemed titles of Guardian of Rhythm and Magic and God of Life. 

Panos embodies the primal forces of creation and vitality, believing that rhythm and melody are the heartbeat of existence. His worshippers, though fewer, are deeply devoted to preserving and reviving ancient dances and songs long forgotten. 

In a twist of destiny, for the same reason he is considered the god of magic and order, so things are kept in the natural order of existence.
:
However, Panos is known for his capricious nature and faltering memory, often loses items of sentimental or magical significance. 

Devoted worshippers embark on quests to find these lost relics, believing that each recovery restores part of Panos’s divine memory and power. 

Miracles performed by Panos frequently begin with the discovery of such items, renewing fervour among his followers.

These zealots, as fanatic as they are, hide their faces with masks that resemble the sun, as they believe Panos brings it every day to bring light and clarity upon mortals.

## The Abbey of Brenadette
The Goddess of the Neverender is seen as tetchy and temperamental, as her job as preserver of the Cycle of Life keeps her always awake. 

That might explain why the most fervent cultists praise the word of Brenadette with unyielding devotion. Their practices are shrouded in mystery, governed by strict rules and marked by the occasional blood sacrifice. These rituals, often conducted in secret, are believed to invoke Brenadette's favour and power.

In their quest to appease Brenadette and harness her might, her followers are known for their unwavering resolve. They will not hesitate to perform sacrifices, believing such acts can calm a raging storm or draw schools of fish closer to the shores. 

To them, each drop of bloodshed is a testament to their faith and a plea for Brenadette’s intervention.
:
The largest Abbey dedicated to her stands in Pharoes, a city frequently battered by stormy weather. 

The locals, living under the constant threat of tempests, sometimes revere Brenadette not only as a strict deity but also as the Goddess of the Tempest. 

Her influence is seen in every thunderclap and flash of lightning that illuminates the skies above.

The last human city is hidden inside **the Lord of Carbohyrr**, the mountain fortress within the deepest mines and hottest forges, with famous hot baths and unparallel craftsmanship. Often with a clear future as a weaponsmith, Carbohyrrians are stoic and with a cold approach for such a warm home.

#### The power
The Duke is chosen every 2 years by vote, with every taxpayer holding one vote. The Battlemages are a structure of trained policemen with magic powers, and they hold the power to keep things in order in this democratic voting, as they keep account of the tax money.

## Culture

### Technology
From the long aqueducts slicing through the landscape, to the high furnaces using magma heat source, the technology is very different between the cities and zones of Galluvinchia.
:
Technology involving heat, water pressure and wind pressure can be seen mainly inside the Lord of Carbohyrr. But the same engineering principles applied there can't be easily moved to other cities, and so on. In Lorda Gorda, they change many of these to be fuelled by the electricity coming from the electric pink jewels.
:
### Language
Everyone speaks the same language, and to greet themselves, instead of ‘hello’, they use the word χαῖρε (pronounced HE-re) for individuals, or χαίρετε (HE-re-te) for groups. 

**he** *(he* r) - **re** (tou *re* tte) - **te** (*te* nant)

### Beyond religion
Unlike the sun, the moon of Galluvinchia never moves. Scholars have debated this for centuries without conclusion. Same with the borders of the world, a limitless ocean without boundaries.

In Galluvinchia, regardless of their religion, their inhabitants have two very decisive beliefs, shared among all of them.

#### Natural rights
In order for a clear moral system, everyone knows their natural rights: 
**Life**:: The right to live and protect oneself.
**Liberty**:: Freedom from arbitrary restraint.
**Property**:: The right to be able to own and enjoy possessions.

These rights are considered universal, predating even the gods themselves, a gift from the original creators that transcends religious belief.

#### Cycle life-death
Galluvinchians, when they die, their free soul will stay a bit in the surroundings of their corpse, resolving their last wishes. 

But when no resentment is kept or after a proper burial, their soul will travel to the Neverender to be part of the Renewal, and become anew, coming back to the living in a new body.
:

### Calendar
Galluvinchia calendar is made of 7-day weeks, with 4 weeks every month, 12 months every year. So every year has 48 weeks or 336 days.

The seventh day is considered the Light day or the Family day and, in many cases, it is used to celebrate it with your dear ones. Months are named after their position in the calendar, from the First to the Eleventh, naming the last one, the Last.

### Coinage
One of the features of this long campaign is the involvement of a biggest, and more impactful, economy driven game. Economy drives many people's lives and interests, and it helps shapes the world. Much as the Roman Empire had their own coinage, and the fall of their greatness can be measured in the devaluation of their minted coins, the world of Galluvinchia can suffer from inflation, deflation, and all other economic fluctuations. This might add a different and more interesting twist to the adventure.

{{descriptive
Are we going to continue carrying scales? Being scammed by the evil? Alchemists, smiths, and wizards? We want to embrace a standard of silver and gold, and populate that the world of Galluvinchia, so we all know the worth of a dinar and an aureo.
This will help commerce, this will help trust. 

Because nothing is faster than the speed of trust.

-- Midas Goldenboy
}}
In most regions, the common currency in use is the dinarii, produced by the prestigious Bank of An’Ramoda. "Dinar" is the singular of "dinarii".

The standard exchange rate is 100 dinarii for 1 aureo, and there are quarters and halves of dinarii.

\column
**At the table,** I know there might be some pain points, and you can keep this feature out of your sessions, but I suggest you keep this in order to keep a realistic point of view in the campaign. 
There is a chance of adventurers finding themselves swimming in money very early in the game, or taking some spells for granted, but with this small change and the updated tables, I think it helps to levelling down their ease to access riches.



I acknowledge that it might be a bit more difficult for people using digital content, in that case it would be interesting to treat dinarii as copper and aureos as gold coins, even if dinarii are made of silver.

#### Why doing this change?
**Realism in pricing**:: Making prices more logical and adaptable to different towns and markets, reflecting real-life economic variations.
**Historical context**:: Reference to historical coinage systems provides a strong foundation for the currency. Establishing the dinar as a product of An’Ramoda gives it significance and suggests a centralized economic power, which adds depth to the political and social landscape of the campaign.

## Magic
Magic in Galluvinchia originates from various sources, yet they all stem from the same fundamental force: the Ripple, a current that washes all beings with magic.

**Wizards** can learn to sense the surge of the Ripple and, if they are attuned to it, they can manage to cast spells by harnessing its stream.


**Sorcerers** innately can feel the rhythm of the Ripple, either by their heritage or by some event that made them sensitive to it.


**Warlocks** can achieve this power by a Patron that funnel the surge of the Ripple into them.

It is essential for **bards** or other magical affined to summon the rhythm of the Ripple in their chants, casts or spell-songs.
:
But might be slightly different for those who draw their power from Nature, **druids** or **rangers**, receiving their powers by synchronizing themselves with the Ripple of the Will of the Wild: a mischievous and twisted being that grants the forests their pulsating life.
:
**Clerics** derive their powers from the gods they worship or from the Light itself, should they choose to revere it.
:
And obviously, **Paladins**. Even if they are religiously inclined, the Ripple finds their volition attractive, and in this case they nurture this power by their oath itself.
:
{{descriptive 
The source of the Ripple remains a mystery, yet it is said that those who are highly attuned to its presence, or who spend prolonged periods immersed in its sound, perceive the voice of an anvil and the clink of a hammer, forging this stream of magic.
}}

The Ripple, sometimes whimsical, sometimes faddy, requires of silver and gold coins, dinarii and aureos, to be able to be taunted into providing more power that strong spells might require.

\column
### The Academies
It is not strange to find magic books in the shelves of many libraries, even in the shelves of some homes. Such is the curiosity that many kids apply for the different academies and ask their parents to support their decision, as it is usually expensive and not within reach for everyone.

#### Academy of Magic Wonders
Located in the Lady of Marmaros, it is the most exclusive scholarship there is. Presided by Magister Monica Mars, it is one of the places where wizard apprentices can become multidisciplinary or, at least, manage to get a taste of all different schools of magic. It holds many scholars that are considered some of the most brilliant minds in the world.

This academy is clearly one of the main powers, not only in Marmaros, but in Galluvinchia, and you can see part of their influence in the many potions, lotions, and products that carry the brand of the academy.

#### Academy of <br>  Magic Waves and Dreams
Situated on the cliffs of Doormi, this academy is considered second to the one in Marmaros due to its more humble focus. Led by Merrion Meyer, it seeks to make magic accessible to many, often offering the first year of study free for brilliant students.
  

#### Academy of Infinite Travels
In the Febris Gulf lie the ruins of an old academy. Long forgotten asnd frequently used as a hideout, this place holds the memories of spells long forgotten and stories yet to unfold.

#### The Library of  <br> Eternal Enlightenment
Close to the castle of the Maneless in Lorda Gorda lies the Library of Eternal Enlightenment. It houses numerous books, some over 500 years old and in unknown languages. The abundance of medical texts led to the establishment of a section dedicated to medical research.

#### Learning spells 

Wizards can copy a spell from a scroll into their spellbook as a downtime activity.
It takes 2 hours per spell level and costs 35 aureos  per spell level, but only 20 aureos per spell level if you have witnessed the cast of this spell before.

Once copied, the spell is permanently in the spellbook and can be prepared like any other wizard spell.


#### Magic schools
The magic schools are always a bit complex, so I will try to make it clearer, assign colours to each one of them so it is easy for anyone detecting magic or any description that is needed.

##### {{color:gold █}} Abjuration 
Protective spells, that shield your allies or creates a barrier. Colour yellow or gold.

##### {{color:MediumAquamarine █}} Conjuration 
Tapping into the elemental forces, bringing allies or summoning materials, or moving things around. Colour dark turquoise.

##### {{color:LightSteelBlue █}} Divination 
Learn secrets, find items and gathering information. Colour pale blue.

##### {{color:magenta █}} Enchantment 
Spells that charm the foes or even breaks them and allows you to command them, or hurt them. Mind control. Colour magenta or hard pink.

##### {{color:Brown █}} Evocation 
You can create something from nothing, manipulate energy, like with Eldritch Blast. Colour dark red.

##### {{color:LightSalmon █}} Illusion 
Looking to deceive the senses of your opponents, and create something afar from the truth. Colour orange or light salmon.

##### {{color:SpringGreen █}} Necromancy
Death manipulation. Colour green.

##### {{color:SteelBlue █}} Transmutation 
They alter the matter, changing the conditions of enemies or items. Colour dark blue.

#### Dispel and Detect Magic
I want to add a clarification on how dispel magic works, in the same way as how Detect Magic can be better used.

##### Detect Magic
When the players use *Detect Magic*, it displays these colours on the Magic schools section, but the potency of these colours is directly related to two factors: the power of the source (it is not the same a lantern and the sun) and how cautious or precise they are investigating the source of the effect, so sometimes the situation may require a *Perception* or *Investigation* check, either at the moment, or considering previous checks.

##### Dispel Magic
*Dispel Magic* can also have negative effects, and this is something to have into consideration. Imagine removing the nails of your bookcase, it will fall into pieces. An example can be found in the next spell, that runners from the Fist hold.
